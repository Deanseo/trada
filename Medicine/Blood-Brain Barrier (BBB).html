3. BBB(Blood-Brain Barrier)

The Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) is the selective barrier that separates the blood from the brain's extracellular fluid in the central nervous system (CNS). It's there to protect the brain from harmful substances, while allowing essential nutrients to get into the brain.

Figure 1. Taken from "Cordance Medical"

In general, lipid soluble molecules, which are "oil-friendly", are the let-in substnaces while water-soluble molecules aren't. That being said, some of the drugs happen to be easily crosisng the BBB, while the other kinds of drugs are specifically designed to be.

4. Well-known drugs that enter the BBB with side-effects

4.1. Caffein and coffee

The most loved drug among the people today, arguably I think, is Caffein. Caffein goes through the BBB, blocking adenosine receptors.

When your brain gets adenosine, it makes you calm. However, caffein blocks its receptors. Therefore your brain can't calm, resulting in a state of alertness. Too much caffein can give you excessive alertness, thus giving you a side-effect of insomnia, as follows:

4.2. Adderall

The notorious Adderall also uses a similar mechanism.

It goes thorugh the BBB, directly stimulates the release of dopamine, disrupting the brain's natural reuptake process. That leads to hyper alertness (stronger than caffein to simply block a bit of adenosine receptors).
